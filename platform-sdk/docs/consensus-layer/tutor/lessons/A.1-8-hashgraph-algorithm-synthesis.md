# Hashgraph algorithm synthesis

## Where we are

This is the closing lesson of cluster A.1 (Hashgraph Algorithm). The seven prior lessons each opened one slot on a vertex of the DAG or one phase of `DefaultConsensusEngine.addEvent` and treated the rest as a black box. [A.1-1 the hashgraph DAG](A.1-1-hashgraph-dag.md) defined the data structure; [A.1-2 rounds and witnesses](A.1-2-rounds-and-witnesses.md) wrote *round-created* and the *witness* flag onto every vertex; [A.1-3 strongly-seeing](A.1-3-strongly-seeing.md) opened the per-event, per-member predicate that round assignment and fame voting both lean on; [A.1-4 voting and coin rounds](A.1-4-voting-and-coin-rounds.md) drove first votes, counting votes, and the coin-round escape; [A.1-5 judges](A.1-5-judges.md) collapsed famous witnesses to a unique-per-creator judge set, fixed round-received, and packaged the round into a `ConsensusSnapshot`; [A.1-6 event lifecycle and birth round](A.1-6-event-lifecycle-and-birth-round.md) walked the four lifecycle regions and the two thresholds that gate them; [A.1-7 stale events](A.1-7-stale-events.md) took the post-admission half of that lifecycle and walked the stale-event reporting that goes with it.

This lesson is the cluster-closing walkthrough. It puts every A.1 component on a single picture: one event arrives on `eventInputWire`, the engine runs the full pipeline against the running state, zero or more decided rounds emerge on `consensusRoundOutputWire`, every event the engine made progress on flows to `preconsensusEventOutputWire`, every event the round-advance evicted without consensus flows to `staleEventOutputWire`, and the engine's internal state advances to receive the next event. The next cluster, A.2 (Event Intake), begins at [A.2-1 the validation pipeline](A.2-1-validation-pipeline.md) — the upstream stages that hand events to `eventInputWire` in valid topological order.

## Motivating problem

The seven prior lessons are deliberately layered: each one isolates one concept so the underlying algorithm fits in a reader's head one piece at a time. The cost of that layering is that no single lesson holds the whole shape. A reader who has finished [A.1-7](A.1-7-stale-events.md) knows what each component *does* but may not yet see what each component *consumes from* and *produces for* the others — what becomes input to fame voting, why the linker is the only place the DAG actually lives, why `EventWindow` flows out of `roundDecided` instead of being incremented by the engine, why the stale partition runs after the round-advance and not before.

The synthesis is that picture. Reading the cluster end-to-end, three pieces fall into place at the same time. First, the algorithm has three time scales, and every component fits cleanly into one of them: per-event decoration (rounds A.1-2, strongly-seeing A.1-3), per-decided-round bookkeeping (voting A.1-4, judges A.1-5), and per-cluster-of-rounds lifecycle (event window A.1-6, stale events A.1-7). Second, the engine's state has a single canonical shape — three fields, each owning one time scale's state — and the per-event pipeline is the shortest path that touches them in sequence. Third, the cluster's external contract is small: three output streams plus one `EventWindow` projection. Everything downstream — signed-state management, gossip, the application — consumes that contract; nothing reads consensus-internal state directly.

## Concept

The conceptual files for the cluster are at [`concepts/hashgraph-dag.md`](../../concepts/hashgraph-dag.md), [`concepts/rounds-and-witnesses.md`](../../concepts/rounds-and-witnesses.md), [`concepts/strongly-seeing.md`](../../concepts/strongly-seeing.md), [`concepts/voting.md`](../../concepts/voting.md), [`concepts/coin-rounds.md`](../../concepts/coin-rounds.md), [`concepts/judges.md`](../../concepts/judges.md), [`concepts/birth-round.md`](../../concepts/birth-round.md), [`concepts/event-lifecycle.md`](../../concepts/event-lifecycle.md), and [`concepts/stale-events.md`](../../concepts/stale-events.md). If you have read the prior seven lessons in order, you have already read these. This lesson does not re-introduce them; it stitches them together.

Three framings anchor the synthesis:

- **Three time scales, three engine fields.** Every piece of cluster-A.1 state lives in exactly one of three places on `DefaultConsensusEngine`. The *per-event* state — the DAG of `EventImpl` instances with their parent pointers and memoized round, witness, judge, `lastSee`, and `stronglySeeP` slots — lives in the linker. The *per-decided-round* state — `RoundElections` for the active election round, the running `numConsensus` counter, `lastConsensusTime`, and the `pcesMode` flag — lives in `Consensus` (`ConsensusImpl`). The *per-cluster-of-rounds* state — the future-event buffer and the freeze-round controller — lives in the engine's own fields. Reading the engine constructor at [`DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L80`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L80) is the shortest way to see the partition; reading `addEvent` ([line 104](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L104)) is the shortest way to see how a single event-input traverses all three.
- **One pipeline, two halves.** The pipeline divides cleanly at the point where `consensus.addEvent` returns. The *first half* is per-event work: classify against the running window, link into the DAG, run the algorithm to decorate the new vertex with its round-created and (if a witness) cast votes that may flip a candidate's fame. The *second half* runs only when consensus advanced — at least one round decided — and is per-decided-round work: take the last decided round's `EventWindow`, evict the events that just became ancient (partitioning into consensus-decided and stale on the way out), and release any future-buffered events whose birth round is now eligible. The work-loop at [line 126](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L126) iterates exactly to drain the cascade the second half can produce.
- **The cluster's contract is three streams and one projection.** `consensusRoundOutputWire` carries the rounds that reached consensus. `preconsensusEventOutputWire` carries every event the engine successfully linked, in the order it linked them. `staleEventOutputWire` carries every event that crossed the ancient threshold from inside the DAG without reaching consensus. The fourth thing the cluster produces is not a wire but a field on the `ConsensusRound`: the `EventWindow` that every downstream and the engine's *next* call will use to classify arrivals. The window is the algorithm's projection of where it has reached, fed back into the input side.

A structural note. There is one cross-cutting concern this lesson surfaces that none of the prior seven held responsibility for: the engine's behaviour when `Consensus.waitingForInitJudges()` is true. This is the post-snapshot-load phase covered briefly in [A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md#how-it-works) — the linker is loading events from PCES or from reconnect, and the engine has to recognise which incoming events are judges of a previously decided round before it can resume voting. While the flag is set, `addEvent` returns an empty output, even on events it linked successfully (`DefaultConsensusEngine.java` [lines 134–149](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L134)). The flag flips false the moment the last init judge arrives; on that same call, the engine flushes the queued pre-consensus events and any consensus rounds that fell out during the init walk ([lines 150–171](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L150)). The init-judges phase is the only one in cluster A.1 where the per-event pipeline behaves non-locally, and it is the bridge to the cluster-C lessons on restart and reconnect.

## How it works

Read [`architecture/topics/hashgraph.md#algorithm-in-current-code`](../../architecture/topics/hashgraph.md#algorithm-in-current-code) alongside this section — the topic file lists the same per-event lifecycle steps, in the same order, with the per-step depth deferred to concepts. This section walks the steps as a single causal chain and names which prior lesson owns each one.

The entry point is `DefaultConsensusEngine.addEvent(event)` at [`DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L104`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L104). The call is invariant: the same method handles the steady-state input stream, PCES replay events, and reconnect-driven re-ingestion. The wired component `DefaultHashgraphModule` at [`DefaultHashgraphModule.java#L68`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultHashgraphModule.java#L68) binds it to `eventInputWire` and fans `ConsensusEngineOutput` out across the three output wires by transformer-and-splitter pairs.

**Step 1 — freeze short-circuit.** Line [107](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L107). `freezeRoundController.isFrozen()` short-circuits to an empty output once the freeze round has emitted. The freeze controller is the per-cluster-of-rounds machinery covered in cluster D (Coordinated network events); from cluster A.1's perspective it is a Boolean valve that closes once and stays closed.

**Step 2 — future cutoff.** Line [112](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L112). `futureEventBuffer.addEvent(event)` returns the event unchanged if it is admissible, `null` if it is ancient or if it is in the future and was filed for later. This is the *future* side of the lifecycle from [A.1-6](A.1-6-event-lifecycle-and-birth-round.md) — the ancient drop in this branch is a redundant guard that lets the engine avoid entering the work loop for an event the linker would also drop.

**Step 3 — work loop start.** Line [126](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L126). The loop drains an `eventsToAdd` queue seeded with the future-cleared event. The queue may grow as the loop runs — the future-buffer release at the end of each iteration appends newly-admissible events — so a single `addEvent` call can process many events.

**Step 4 — link.** Line [128](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L128). `linker.linkEvent(eventToAdd)` is the second ancient guard ([A.1-6](A.1-6-event-lifecycle-and-birth-round.md)) and the actual DAG insertion ([A.1-1](A.1-1-hashgraph-dag.md)). Ancient events return `null` and the loop `continue`s. Admitted events are wrapped into an `EventImpl`, parent pointers are resolved by hash, and the event is added to both the `birthRound`-keyed descriptor map and the `Hash`-keyed map.

**Step 5 — init-judges gate (entering).** Line [135](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L135). The engine snapshots `consensus.waitingForInitJudges()` *before* the algorithm call. After a snapshot load (`outOfBandSnapshotUpdate`, [line 202](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L202)), this flag is true and stays true until enough events have flowed in for `InitJudges` to recognise every judge of the snapshot's last decided round. Reading the *before* and *after* values is what tells the engine, on a single event, whether it just crossed the transition.

**Step 6 — algorithm processing.** Line [137](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L137). `consensus.addEvent(linkedEvent)` runs the per-event algorithm pipeline inside `ConsensusImpl`. This is the largest single piece of cluster A.1 — it covers everything from [A.1-2](A.1-2-rounds-and-witnesses.md) through [A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md):

1. `ConsensusImpl.round(event)` computes round-created ([A.1-2](A.1-2-rounds-and-witnesses.md), code at [`ConsensusImpl.java#L1120`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/consensus/ConsensusImpl.java#L1120)). The case-4 strongly-seeing check ([A.1-3](A.1-3-strongly-seeing.md), code at [`ConsensusImpl.java#L1054`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/consensus/ConsensusImpl.java#L1054)) decides whether the event bumps to the next round.
2. `ConsensusImpl.witness(event)` ([A.1-2](A.1-2-rounds-and-witnesses.md), code at [`ConsensusImpl.java#L912`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/consensus/ConsensusImpl.java#L912)) flags the event a witness iff its round exceeds its self-parent's. If true, the engine adds the event to `RoundElections` via `rounds.newWitness(event)` when its round equals the election round, or invokes `voteInAllElections(event)` when its round is greater.
3. `voteInAllElections` ([A.1-4](A.1-4-voting-and-coin-rounds.md), code at [`ConsensusImpl.java#L506`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/consensus/ConsensusImpl.java#L506)) walks every undecided candidate witness in the election round and casts this voter's vote — first vote at `diff == 1`, counting vote at `diff > 1`, coin-round override at `diff % coinFreq == 0`. Each counting vote that clears `Threshold.SUPER_MAJORITY` flips a candidate to decided famous (or decided not-famous).
4. If `RoundElections.isDecided()` flips true, `roundDecided` ([A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md), code at [`ConsensusImpl.java#L706`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/consensus/ConsensusImpl.java#L706)) runs the famous-to-judge collapse via `RoundElections.findAllJudges` (lex-min on base hash for branched creators), generates the per-round whitening byte string via `ConsensusUtils.generateWhitening`, walks `search.commonAncestorsOf(judges, …)` to fix round-received on the events that this round closes, runs `ConsensusSorter.sort` to put them in consensus order, and packages the result into a `ConsensusRound` carrying the consensus event list, the new `EventWindow`, a `ConsensusSnapshot`, the active roster, the `pcesMode` flag, and a creation timestamp.

The return value is a `List<ConsensusRound>` — zero, one, or several depending on what the future-buffer cascade may have queued up earlier in the same call. The engine appends them to `allConsensusRounds`.

**Step 7 — init-judges gate (exiting).** Lines [139–171](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L139). The engine reads `waitingForInitJudges()` *after* the algorithm call. If still true, return empty — the engine does not classify the just-linked event as pre-consensus because that event may have been part of a round decided before the snapshot, and re-emitting it as pre-consensus would double-count it. If just-flipped-to-false on this call, the engine flushes every consensus event of every decided round and every queued pre-consensus event from the consensus side, so the post-init output mirrors what steady-state operation would have produced. If false on both before and after, normal steady-state: append the just-added event to the pre-consensus list ([line 170](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L170)).

**Step 8 — window advance.** Lines [177–188](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L177). Only when at least one round decided this iteration. The engine takes the *last* round's `EventWindow` ([line 179](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L179)) — the most advanced window the consensus side produced — and runs two coupled operations against it. `linker.setEventWindow(eventWindow)` ([line 183](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L183), [A.1-6](A.1-6-event-lifecycle-and-birth-round.md)) shifts the DAG's `parentDescriptorMap` window forward to the new ancient threshold, removes the evicted descriptors from `parentHashMap`, calls `EventImpl.clear()` on each, and returns the list of newly-ancient `EventImpl` instances. The engine partitions that list at [lines 184–187](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L184): events whose `isConsensus()` is true have already left on `consensusRoundOutputWire` and are dropped; the rest are *stale* and are projected to their underlying `PlatformEvent` and accumulated for the stale stream ([A.1-7](A.1-7-stale-events.md)). The `futureEventBuffer.updateEventWindow(eventWindow)` call at [line 188](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L188) releases buffered events whose birth round is now eligible. Released events go back onto `eventsToAdd` and the loop iterates.

**Step 9 — freeze filter and return.** Lines [193–195](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L193). After the work loop drains, `freezeRoundController.filterAndModify(allConsensusRounds)` truncates any rounds past the freeze boundary (cluster D again), the stale events are reported to metrics, and the call returns a `ConsensusEngineOutput(rounds, preConsensusEvents, staleEvents)`. The wired component's three transformer-and-splitter pairs ([`DefaultHashgraphModule.java#L68–80`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultHashgraphModule.java#L68)) fan the three fields out across the three output wires.

The same nine steps run for every event. There is no batch path, no async path, no side-channel for snapshot ingestion: snapshots come in through a separate input wire (`consensusSnapshotInputWire` → `outOfBandSnapshotUpdate` at [line 202](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L202)) that resets the linker, clears the future-event buffer, and reseeds `Consensus` from the snapshot, after which the same `addEvent` pipeline rehydrates the DAG via the init-judges gate.

## Worked example

A node that has been running steadily, the running state is:

- Last decided round: 100. Active election round: 101. `pendingConsensusRound`: 101.
- `EventWindow`: `(latestConsensusRound = 100, newEventBirthRound = 101, ancientThreshold = 90, expiredThreshold = 80)`.
- Future buffer: one buffered event `e_105` (birth round 105, filed earlier).
- Linker: roughly 4N events from the non-ancient slice, including witnesses of rounds 90–101 and a self-event `s_88` of this node, birth round 88, admitted long ago, never reached consensus.
- Roster: four equal-weight nodes A, B, C, D. Super-majority threshold: 3 of 4.
- `RoundElections` for round 101: candidates `w101_A`, `w101_B`, `w101_C`, `w101_D`; `w101_A`, `w101_B`, `w101_C` already decided famous; `w101_D` still undecided.

A new event `e_102` arrives — a round-102 witness by D, birth round 102. The pipeline runs.

1. **Freeze.** `isFrozen()` → false. Continue.
2. **Future buffer.** `futureEventBuffer.addEvent(e_102)`: `102 <= 101 + 1 = 102` is true under `getMaximumReleasableRound`, so admissible. Returns `e_102` unchanged.
3. **Work loop, iteration 1.** `eventsToAdd = [e_102]`.
4. **Link.** `linker.linkEvent(e_102)`: not ancient (`102 >= 90`); parents resolved through `parentHashMap`; inserted into both maps as `linkedEvent: EventImpl`.
5. **Init-judges before-snapshot.** `waitingForInitJudges()` → false (steady-state).
6. **Algorithm processing.** `consensus.addEvent(linkedEvent)`:
   - `ConsensusImpl.round(linkedEvent)`: parents agree on round 101; the function walks `timedStronglySeeP` over the four roster members for `linkedEvent`'s seen round-101 witnesses; super-majority of weight reached; bumps to 102. `linkedEvent.setRoundCreated(102)`.
   - `ConsensusImpl.witness(linkedEvent)`: round 102 ≠ self-parent's round 101; flag set true. `linkedEvent.setWitness(true)`. The engine registers `linkedEvent` for round 102 via `rounds.newWitness` and invokes `voteInAllElections(linkedEvent)`.
   - `voteInAllElections(linkedEvent)`: `diff = 102 − 101 = 1`. The first-vote branch fires for the one undecided candidate `w101_D`. `firstVote(linkedEvent, w101_D)`: walks `firstSee(linkedEvent, D.rosterIndex)` and back through `firstSelfWitnessS` to find the first round-101 D-witness on `linkedEvent`'s self-ancestor chain. Suppose that is `w101_D` itself — `firstVote` returns true. `linkedEvent.setVote(w101_D, true)`. First votes never decide; the candidate stays undecided.
   - `RoundElections.isDecided()` → false. `consensus.addEvent` returns the empty list.
7. **Init-judges after-snapshot.** `waitingForInitJudges()` → false. Steady-state branch fires: `preConsensusEvents.add(linkedEvent.getBaseEvent())`.
8. **Window advance.** `allConsensusRounds.isEmpty()` → true; `continue`. The work loop has no more events; loop exits.
9. **Return.** `staleEvents` is empty. `freezeRoundController.filterAndModify([])` returns empty. The output is `ConsensusEngineOutput([], [e_102], [])` — no consensus rounds, one pre-consensus event, no stale events.

Now suppose the next event to arrive is `e_103` — a round-103 witness by A whose first-vote and counting-vote paths happen to be the trigger that flips `w101_D` to decided famous. (The exact path is: round-103 witnesses cast counting votes over their strongly-seen round-102 witnesses; `e_103` sees a super-majority of round-102 witnesses that voted YES on `w101_D`; the counting vote returns `YES_MAJORITY` with `supermajority == true`; `candidateWitness.fameDecided(true)` flips `w101_D`.) Trace the same nine steps:

1–5. Identical to before; `e_103` arrives, future-clears, links into the DAG, init-judges flag false.

6. `consensus.addEvent`:
   - `round(e_103) = 103` (parents agree on 102; super-majority of round-102 witnesses strongly seen).
   - `witness(e_103) = true`.
   - `voteInAllElections(e_103)`: `diff = 103 − 101 = 2`. The counting branch fires. `getStronglySeenInPreviousRound(e_103)` returns `[v102_A, v102_B, v102_C, v102_D]`. `getCountingVote(w101_D, strongly_seen)` walks each witness's prior vote, tallies YES-weight 4 and NO-weight 0; super-majority is true; the returned `CountingVote` is `YES_MAJORITY`. `isCoinRound(2) = 2 % 12 == 0` → false. Normal counting branch: `e_103.setVote(w101_D, true)`; `w101_D.fameDecided(true)`. Every candidate in round 101 is now decided. `RoundElections.isDecided() = true`.
   - `roundDecided(rounds.getElectionRound())` runs ([A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md)). `findAllJudges` collapses `{w101_A, w101_B, w101_C, w101_D}` to itself (no branching) and sorts by creator id: `[w101_A, w101_B, w101_C, w101_D]`. `setJudgeTrue` flips the flag on each. `checkJudges` sums roster weights to 4, clears super-majority, logs nothing. `generateWhitening` XOR-folds the four base hashes into `W`. `findConsensusEvents` runs `search.commonAncestorsOf([w101_A, w101_B, w101_C, w101_D], nonConsensusNonAncient)`, returning the set of admitted-and-not-yet-consensus events that ancestor every judge of round 101 — call this set `R101`. Each event gets `setIsConsensusTrue(e, 101)`. `ConsensusSorter.sort(R101, W)` orders them by `(preliminaryConsensusTimestamp, extendedMedian(recTimes), cGen, arrayCompare(baseHash, baseHash, W))`. The result is packaged into a `ConsensusRound(round = 101, events = R101_sorted, EventWindow = (101, 102, 91, 81), ConsensusSnapshot = (round = 101, minimumJudgeInfo = …, numConsensus, lastConsensusTime, judgeIds = [(A.id, h(w101_A)), (B.id, h(w101_B)), (C.id, h(w101_C)), (D.id, h(w101_D))]), roster, pcesMode = false, time.now())`.
   - `consensus.addEvent` returns `[round-101 ConsensusRound]`.
7. **Init-judges after-snapshot.** Still false. Steady-state branch: `preConsensusEvents.add(e_103.getBaseEvent())`.
8. **Window advance.** `allConsensusRounds.isEmpty() = false`. `eventWindow = (101, 102, 91, 81)`. `linker.setEventWindow(eventWindow)` shifts the descriptor map to ancient threshold 91, evicts every `EventImpl` with birth round below 91, calls `clear()` on each. Suppose the evicted set is `[…, s_88, …]`. The partition at [lines 184–187](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L184): the consensus-decided evicted events leave nothing on the floor; `s_88` is not consensus, projected to its base event, appended to `staleEvents`. `futureEventBuffer.updateEventWindow(eventWindow)` shifts the buffer's release window to `pendingConsensusRound + 1 = 103`; `e_105` is still in the future (`105 >= 103`), stays buffered; the buffer returns an empty list. The work loop has no more events; loop exits.
9. **Return.** `staleEvents = [s_88.getBaseEvent()]`. `freezeRoundController.filterAndModify([round-101])` returns `[round-101]`. `consensusEngineMetrics.reportStaleEvent(s_88.getBaseEvent())` runs and the self-event check inside the metrics handler increments the stale counter (creator is local). The output is `ConsensusEngineOutput([round-101], [e_103], [s_88])`.

Downstream, `DefaultHashgraphModule`'s transformer-and-splitter pairs unpack the tuple. `consensusRoundOutputWire` emits the round-101 `ConsensusRound`; signed-state management ([cluster C](../../tutor/lessons/C-1-signed-state-lifecycle.md)) consumes it. `preconsensusEventOutputWire` emits `e_103`; the pre-consensus path feeds gossip's shadowgraph and the signed-state pre-hash. `staleEventOutputWire` emits `s_88`; the application's registered callback gets a one-`PlatformEvent` notification and may resubmit `s_88`'s transactions on a fresh self-event ([A.1-7](A.1-7-stale-events.md)).

Two corollaries the example surfaces. First, the engine emitted a round in one event's pipeline (`e_103`) and emitted a stale event in the *same* pipeline. The round-advance and the stale partition are coupled: every round that decides may evict events, and every event evicted without consensus becomes a stale-event report on the same `addEvent` call. The two output streams correlate over time, even though they are independent wires. Second, the future buffer never released anything in this trace, but if `e_103`'s round-advance had pushed `pendingConsensusRound` to `106`, `e_105` would have been released, re-entered the work loop, and possibly triggered another round-decision in the same `addEvent` call. The cascade is what the work-loop drains.

## Delta callout

The `delta-map/` catalog has not populated for the hashgraph topic, so there is no `delta-map/hashgraph.md` to reference (the same open question every prior A.1 lesson flagged). The relevant proposal-level changes, from the topic file's [Future state section](../../architecture/topics/hashgraph.md#future-state), are two:

First, the cluster's external contract is restructured. The proposal envisions a single public `Consensus` API on which the Execution Layer calls `nextRound(roster)` to pull each round, giving natural backpressure; today the boundary is `consensusRoundOutputWire` plus a split across `ExecutionLayer` and `ConsensusStateEventHandler` in `swirlds-platform-core`. The change is at the boundary, not the algorithm: the same nine-step pipeline this lesson walked stays intact. Only the way downstream consumers acquire the resulting `ConsensusRound` changes, and the wiring-framework-driven fan-out via transformer-and-splitter pairs in `DefaultHashgraphModule` is what would be retired in favour of the explicit `nextRound` pull.

Second, the proposal introduces a separate *Sheriff* module that aggregates misbehavior reports — branched-creator events, malformed parents, signature failures. The Sheriff is not present in the current `consensus-hashgraph` module or anywhere else in the code. The cluster's stance on misbehavior today is implicit: branched-creator events are admitted to the DAG and handled by the algorithm's existing Byzantine-safety mechanisms ([A.1-3](A.1-3-strongly-seeing.md), [A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md)); the resulting consensus is correct, and the cost is paid in voting latency rather than in consensus violation ([A.1-4](A.1-4-voting-and-coin-rounds.md) worked example, [A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md) worked example). A reader looking inside `consensus-hashgraph` for a Sheriff will not find it; misbehavior in current code is absorbed, not reported.

Neither change affects the algorithm — round assignment, strongly-seeing, voting, judges, the lifecycle staircase, and the stale-event report all carry forward unchanged. The proposed redesign is a boundary change with a misbehavior side-channel addition, not an algorithm change.

## Cluster worked example

This section is the synthesis the cluster has been building toward — a single narrative walk through the algorithm with every cluster-A.1 component named explicitly. Pick up a four-node network (A, B, C, D, equal weight) just after node B has booted, loaded a snapshot at decided round 50, and is starting to receive events through `eventInputWire`. The snapshot carried `judgeIds = [(A.id, h(j50_A)), (B.id, h(j50_B)), (C.id, h(j50_C)), (D.id, h(j50_D))]`. `Consensus.loadSnapshot` seeded an `InitJudges` instance with these four hashes; `Consensus.waitingForInitJudges()` returns `true`.

**The init-judges phase.** The first events that arrive on `eventInputWire` are the historical events flowing back from PCES replay ([cluster C](../../tutor/lessons/C-3-inline-pces-write-path.md)). Each one's `addEvent` call:

- Frees through the future buffer (the buffer's window is set from the snapshot's `EventWindow`).
- Links into the `ConsensusLinker`'s two parallel maps ([A.1-1](A.1-1-hashgraph-dag.md)).
- Hits step 5 — `waitingForInitJudges()` is true. The engine still calls `consensus.addEvent(linkedEvent)`, which runs `ConsensusImpl.addEvent`. The witness and round-assignment paths from [A.1-2](A.1-2-rounds-and-witnesses.md) and [A.1-3](A.1-3-strongly-seeing.md) execute as normal — round-created is set, the witness flag is computed, the per-event slots are populated. But before any output is produced, [`ConsensusImpl.addEvent` at line 388](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/consensus/ConsensusImpl.java#L388) checks whether the linked event's hash is in `InitJudges`'s expected set. If yes, `InitJudges.judgeFound` flips the event's witness, famous, fame-decided, and judge flags in one step, *without* invoking `voteInAllElections`; the judge state of round 50 is rehydrated from the on-disk record rather than recomputed.
- After the algorithm returns, the engine's step-7 check sees `waitingForInitJudges()` is *still* true and returns empty. No pre-consensus output, no consensus rounds. The event was admitted to the DAG and may already carry its judge flag, but the engine does not advertise it to the wires.

This phase continues for as many PCES events as it takes to land on the fourth judge of round 50. On that event's `addEvent` call, `InitJudges.numMissingJudges()` reaches zero, `waitingForInitJudges()` flips false. Step 7's before-true-after-false branch fires: every consensus event from every round decided during init walks (typically zero rounds, but possibly some if PCES carried events past round 50) and every pre-consensus event accumulated inside `Consensus.getPreConsensusEvents()` is flushed to the output. The engine emerges into steady state with the DAG fully reconstructed back to ancient threshold, the running consensus state aligned to round 50's judges, and the next event ready to be classified.

**The steady-state phase.** Events now flow from gossip ([cluster A.3](../../tutor/lessons/A.3-1-protocol-stack-and-rpc-sync.md)) and from the local event creator ([cluster A.4](../../tutor/lessons/A.4-1-tipset-algorithm.md)). Each one runs the full nine-step pipeline. Take round 51's evolution as the case study:

- The first round-51 events to arrive are witnesses — one per creator in the well-mixed case. Each is `ConsensusImpl.round`-bumped from 50 to 51 because it strongly sees a super-majority of round-50 witnesses ([A.1-2](A.1-2-rounds-and-witnesses.md) case 4, [A.1-3](A.1-3-strongly-seeing.md) `stronglySeeP`); each is flagged a witness ([A.1-2](A.1-2-rounds-and-witnesses.md) `ConsensusImpl.witness`); each is registered with `RoundElections` for round 51 because round 51 is the active election round.
- Round-52 witnesses begin to arrive. Each one calls `voteInAllElections` with `diff = 1`; each casts a first vote on each of the four round-51 candidates by walking its self-witness chain ([A.1-4](A.1-4-voting-and-coin-rounds.md) `firstVote`). First votes seed the next layer; nothing decides yet.
- Round-53 witnesses arrive. Each one calls `voteInAllElections` with `diff = 2`; each fetches its strongly-seen round-52 witnesses via `getStronglySeenInPreviousRound` ([A.1-3](A.1-3-strongly-seeing.md)), tallies their first votes via `getCountingVote`, and — because the honest first votes are uniform — clears `Threshold.SUPER_MAJORITY` on every candidate with `YES_MAJORITY`. The voting at `diff = 2` decides every round-51 candidate famous on the first round-53 witness to land that closes its tally.
- `RoundElections.isDecided()` flips true. `addEvent` reads the post-vote flag inside `ConsensusImpl.addEvent`, and `roundDecided(rounds.getElectionRound())` runs ([A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md)). `findAllJudges` collapses the famous set; with no branching, the judge set is the famous set sorted by creator id. `checkJudges` confirms super-majority weight. `generateWhitening` builds the per-round whitening byte string. `findConsensusEvents` walks `commonAncestorsOf(judges, nonConsensusNonAncient)` and returns the events that ancestor every round-51 judge — `R51`. Each event in `R51` gets `setIsConsensusTrue(e, 51)`. `ConsensusSorter.sort(R51, whitening)` orders them. A `ConsensusRound` for round 51 is constructed and returned to the engine.
- Back in `DefaultConsensusEngine.addEvent`, step 8 fires. `eventWindow = newConsensusRound.getEventWindow() = (latestConsensusRound = 51, newEventBirthRound = 52, ancientThreshold = 41, expiredThreshold = 31)`. `linker.setEventWindow(eventWindow)`: the `parentDescriptorMap`'s window shifts to 41; every `EventImpl` with birth round in `[…, 41)` is evicted, `clear()`-ed, returned as the `ancientEvents` list. The partition at [`DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L184`](https://github.com/hashgraph/hiero-consensus-node/blob/main/platform-sdk/consensus-hashgraph-impl/src/main/java/org/hiero/consensus/hashgraph/impl/DefaultConsensusEngine.java#L184) drops the `isConsensus()`-true entries and accumulates the rest into `staleEvents` ([A.1-7](A.1-7-stale-events.md)). `futureEventBuffer.updateEventWindow(eventWindow)` shifts the future-buffer's window to `pendingConsensusRound + 1 = 53`; events buffered with birth round in `[…, 53)` are released into `eventsToAdd` and the work loop iterates against them — possibly cascading into another round-decision if those buffered events were the ones gating round 52's voting.

**The three output streams.** Over the course of this work loop, three things have flowed out:

- `consensusRoundOutputWire`: the round-51 `ConsensusRound`, carrying `R51` in consensus order, the `EventWindow` that the next event-input will be classified against, the `ConsensusSnapshot` whose `judgeIds` are the persisted handle for the next restart/reconnect, and the `pcesMode` flag (false in steady state). Signed-state management ([cluster C](../../tutor/lessons/C-1-signed-state-lifecycle.md)) consumes this. The snapshot is what will let *any* node — this one or a fresh peer joining via reconnect — replay round 51 without re-running fame voting from the beginning of history.
- `preconsensusEventOutputWire`: every event the engine successfully linked across the work loop's iterations, in link order. Gossip's shadowgraph consumes this for catch-up service to peers; the signed-state pre-hash consumes it for early hashing of events that will eventually carry transactions into a consensus round.
- `staleEventOutputWire`: the events the round-advance evicted without consensus. These are the events that were admitted to the DAG in some earlier round, never reached consensus, and have just been unlinked. The application's registered callback ([A.1-7](A.1-7-stale-events.md)) receives each one; self-events flagged through this wire are the application's signal that its transactions need resubmission.

**The contract.** Step back from the trace. What the cluster guarantees to the rest of the platform is:

1. Every event linked into the DAG either reaches consensus and is emitted on `consensusRoundOutputWire`, or it ages out and is emitted on `staleEventOutputWire`. No event vanishes silently after admission. (Caveat from [A.1-7](A.1-7-stale-events.md): events that age out in the gap of a restart or reconnect are not reported, because the in-memory linker that does the partition was not present to observe the transition.)
2. Every event linked into the DAG is also emitted on `preconsensusEventOutputWire` exactly once, in link order. (Caveat: during the init-judges phase, the pre-consensus emission is deferred; events linked under `waitingForInitJudges() == true` are flushed in the `addEvent` call that flips the flag false.)
3. Every `ConsensusRound` emitted carries an `EventWindow` whose thresholds advance monotonically across calls. The next event arriving will be classified against the most recent emitted `EventWindow`.
4. The contents and order of each `ConsensusRound` are a deterministic function of the DAG content. Two honest nodes with the same DAG produce byte-identical consensus rounds — including the byte ordering of consensus events within the round, broken by the whitened tie-break that [A.1-5](A.1-5-judges.md) defined.

Those four properties are the cluster's contribution to the consensus layer. Everything else in cluster A.1 is the machinery that delivers them.
